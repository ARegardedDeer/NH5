# Nimehime User Motivation Model
_Last updated: 2025-10-18_

## 1. Core Assumptions (User Desires)

| # | User Wants To... | Context |
|---|------------------|----------|
| 1 | Search information about anime. | Use search or Discover tab. |
| 2 | See Youtubers’ or influencers’ top lists. | Social proof & curiosity. |
| 3 | Get recommendations (system or peers). | Home / Discover. |
| 4 | Receive recommendations from other players. | Peer validation loop. |
| 5 | Rate & maintain a watchlist. | Contribution & identity. |

---

## 2. App Goals (System Desires)

| # | App Wants User To... | Why |
|---|----------------------|------|
| 1 | Provide ratings/reviews (data input). | Cold-start mitigation. |
| 2 | Return daily or frequently. | Retention & habit building. |
| 3 | Engage with community (posts/comments). | Network density & stickiness. |

---

## 3. Behavioral Design Bridge

| Category | UX Mechanism | Feedback Tier | Example |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|----------|
| **Data Contribution** | Rating quests, rail-end prompts | Tier 1–2 | “Rate 3 to unlock Horror badge.” |
| **Retention** | Daily streaks & XP summary | Tier 3–4 | “🔥 Day 3 streak! +20 bonus EXP” |
| **Community** | Discussion highlights, badges | Tier 2–3 | “Your comment got 5 upvotes → +15 EXP!” |

---

## 4. Emotional Map

| Stage | Emotion | UX Support |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Onboarding** | Curiosity | “Explore your first anime worlds.” |
| **Engagement** | Flow | Combo & quest feedback loops. |
| **Retention** | Pride | XP bar, badges, streaks. |
| **Advocacy** | Identity | Community presence, leaderboard. |

---

## 5. Success Metrics (KPIs)

| Category | Metric | Target |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Data Density | Avg. ratings/session | ≥ 3 |
| Retention | D7 retention | ≥ 35% |
| Engagement | Comments per active user | ≥ 1.5 |
| Satisfaction | Avg. session time | ≥ 5 min |

---

## 6. Summary

The Nimehime motivation model blends **RPG-like progression**, **social validation**, and **structured discovery**.  
Every user action either refines personalization or reinforces identity — both feeding back into long-term engagement.
